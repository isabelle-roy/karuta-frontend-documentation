The Share metadata is used to allow the user to share part or all of their portfolio with a third party. It is also possible to give this third party the right to edit shared elements. The user shares his or her portfolio by clicking on a button, which triggers an email containing a hyperlink to the shared elements to be sent to the third party.

User sharing enables two scenarios:

  • notify one of the portfolio users that they need to intervene;
  • allow a user who does not currently have the rights to intervene in a portfolio to do so.

In the Portfolio

In this example, when the student clicks on "Request peer review", the system sends an email to the peer with a hyperlink to a section of the portfolio in which they can enter their comment.

There are multiple options for the security conditions of user sharing; here are some common use-cases:

Use case 1 — The student can show his or her portfolio to anyone (Karuta user or not) in read-only mode (no edit rights).

student,all,?,4,unlimited,Share@en

Note 1: Using the ? symbol, the recipient is not predetermined by the designer; when the student clicks on Share, a pop-up window will allow him or her to specify the recipient's email.

Note 2: The portfolio instance must be shared with the user public in the role all.

Use case 2 — The student can show his portfolio to anyone (Karuta user or not) in read-only mode < (no edit rights), but the sharing has a limited duration of 24 hours.

student,all,?,4,24,Share@en

Note 1: Using the ? symbol, the recipient is not predetermined by the designer; when the student clicks on Share, a pop-up window will allow him or her to specify the recipient's email.

Note 2: The portfolio instance must be shared with the user public in the role all.

Use case 3 — The student requests for his or her teacher to proceed with their evaluation; the teacher has a Karuta account and the student's portfolio is shared with said teacher in the teacher role.

student,teacher,teacher,1,12,Request an evaluation@en

The teacher receives an email and clicks on the hyperlink it contains to proceed with the evaluation.

Use case 4 — The student requests for his or her internship supervisor to proceed with their evaluation; the internship supervisor does not have a Karuta account.

student,supervisor,yves.lacoste@airfrance.com,3,12,Request an evaluation@en

The supervisor receives an email and clicks on the hyperlink it contains to proceed with the evaluation; the supervisor does not have to identify him or herself in Karuta. The hyperlink is valid for 12 hours.

Use case 5 — The student asks a peer to add a review in his or her portfolio; the colleague has a Karuta account.

student,peer,?,1,12,Request peer review@en

The peer receives an email and clicks on the hyperlink it contains to add his comment.

Note: Using the ? symbol, the recipient is not predetermined by the designer; when the student clicks on Request peer review, a pop-up window will allow him or her to specify the recipient's email. It is assumed here that the student has a great number of peers and that the designer did not specify which one will be mobilized, so as to give the student more latitude.

Security Levels

Level Conditions
0 The recipient has a Karuta account and the portfolio is already linked with this account in the correct role. If the recipient is not logged into Karuta he or she will be asked to do so before he can access the portfolio content.
1 The recipient has a Karuta account and the portfolio is already linked with this account in the correct role. If the recipient is not logged into Karuta he or she will NOT have to do so: clicking on the hyperlink will automatically lead he or she into the portfolio.
2 The recipient has a Karuta account but the portfolio is NOT already linked with this account in the correct role (linking will be done on the fly by Karuta). If the recipient is not logged into Karuta he or she will NOT have to do so: clicking on the hyperlink will automatically lead he or she into the portfolio.
3 The recipient does NOT have a Karuta account. A temporary account will be created on the fly by Karuta.
4 The recipient gets a read-only access to the portfolio section being shared. During instanciation of the portfolio, the "all" role must be linked with the user "public".
